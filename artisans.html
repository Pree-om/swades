Rajesh Kumar

Blue Pottery

Jaipur, Rajasthan | 7th Generation Artisan

The Tradition

Blue pottery originated in Persia and was brought to India by Mughal artisans. My family has been practicing this craft in Jaipur for over 200 years.

The Process

Each piece is hand-shaped from a unique blend of quartz, glass, and multani mitti (Fuller's earth), then painted with cobalt oxide before firing at low temperatures.

The Challenge

With mass-produced ceramics flooding the market, we struggle to compete on price while maintaining our traditional methods and quality.

Fatima Begum

Banarasi Weaving

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh | Master Weaver

The Legacy

My family has been weaving Banarasi silks for generations. I learned the craft from my mother at age 12, sitting at the loom for hours each day.

The Art

A single saree can take 15-30 days to complete, with intricate zari work woven with real gold and silver threads. The patterns often depict Mughal motifs and nature.

The Future

I'm teaching my daughters the craft, but many young people leave for city jobs. We need more support to keep this heritage alive.

Arun Mehta

Wood Carving

Srinagar, Kashmir | 4th Generation Craftsman

The Craft

Kashmiri walnut wood carving is unique for its intricate floral patterns and durability. The walnut trees grow slowly in our climate, making the wood precious.

The Technique

Each piece is carved by hand using 32 different chisels. A single decorative box can take 2 weeks to complete, while larger pieces may take months.
